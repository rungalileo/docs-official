---
title: 'Suggested Edits'
description: 'Learn how to enable and manage suggested edits in your documentation'
---

Suggested edits allow your documentation users to propose changes directly through the documentation interface, making it easier to collect feedback and maintain accurate documentation.

## Enabling Suggested Edits

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Project Settings">
    Go to your project dashboard and click on the "Settings" tab in the navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find Collaboration Settings">
    Scroll down to the "Collaboration" section where you'll find the suggested edits configuration.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable the Feature">
    Toggle the "Enable Suggested Edits" switch to turn on the feature for your documentation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Permissions">
    Choose who can make suggestions:
    - All users
    - Authenticated users only
    - Selected user groups
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Making Suggestions as a User

<Card title="How to Suggest an Edit" icon="pen-to-square">
  Once suggested edits are enabled, users will see an "Edit" button on each documentation page. This allows them to propose changes directly through the interface.
</Card>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Edit">
    Look for the edit icon in the top right corner of any documentation page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Your Changes">
    Use the markdown editor to make your proposed changes to the documentation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preview Changes">
    Review your changes in the preview pane to ensure they appear as intended.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit for Review">
    Add a description of your changes and click "Submit Suggestion" to send it for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your suggestions will be reviewed by project administrators before being merged into the main documentation.
</Note>

## Reviewing Suggestions (Admins)

Project owners and administrators can manage suggested edits through the admin dashboard.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Review Queue" icon="list-check">
    Access all pending suggestions in one place through the "Suggested Edits" dashboard.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Diff View" icon="code-compare">
    See exactly what changes are being proposed with side-by-side comparison views.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Review Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Review Dashboard">
    Navigate to the "Suggested Edits" section in your admin dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Evaluate Changes">
    Review the proposed changes and their impact on the documentation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Take Action">
    Choose to:
    - Accept the changes
    - Reject with feedback
    - Request modifications
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Provide Feedback">
    Add comments or feedback for the suggester, especially if requesting changes or rejecting the suggestion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Setting Clear Guidelines" icon="book">
    Create and share clear guidelines for what kinds of suggestions are welcome and how they should be formatted.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Regular Review Schedule" icon="calendar-check">
    Establish a regular schedule for reviewing suggested edits to ensure timely feedback and maintain contributor engagement.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Quality Control" icon="shield-check">
    Implement a checklist for reviewing suggestions:
    - Technical accuracy
    - Grammar and style consistency
    - Documentation standards compliance
    - Link validity
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Communication" icon="comments">
    Maintain clear communication with contributors:
    - Acknowledge submissions promptly
    - Provide constructive feedback
    - Explain rejection reasons
    - Thank contributors for their input
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Remember to regularly backup your documentation before accepting multiple suggested edits, as changes can significantly impact your content.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Consider implementing a reward or recognition system for valuable contributors to encourage high-quality suggestions and active participation in improving the documentation.
</Tip>
